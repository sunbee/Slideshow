Assessment:


Thoughts bubble up in the mind. Our likes or dislikes give rise to feelings. But the mind itself has arisen like a wave cresting on the surface of ocean. So many waves are arising and falling all the time on the surface of the ocean. That ocean of existence is our true nature called brahman.

Rising above the duality takes skill. The skill to shift perception from events to existence. Attachment to the experience of events diminishes awareness. Being centered on awareness diminishes the agitation of events. The thorn has lodged in the body causing irritation. It must be skillfully extricated, mere rubbing will only cause it to lodge in deeper.

Skill is needed to quiet the mind and go deep into oneself, to avoid chewing on an experience, cogitating over it in the mind long after the event has passed. Events arise like bubbles out of changeless existence and dissipate back in it. The Sanskrit word for bubble is budbuda. Experiences pass like than the foamy bubbles tossed on a wave.

Taranga phena budbuda. Wave, foam, bubble.
