Ashtavakra Gita

Ashtavakra tells the story of a man who mistook a rope for a in the feeble light of a lantern at night and was so petrified that he was stood rooted to the spot, until the truth was revealed by light of dawn.

Assessment:


A traveller passing by a forest in the dark night saw a snake on the path in the feeble light of a lantern. Petrified, he stood rooted to the spot for hours, until the truth was revealed by light of dawn. The traveller laughed at his own foolishness. The forest, once dark and intimidating, burst forth with the cadence of life. Birds sang, peacocks danced, streams flowed.

Events that seem bothersome become play with dawning knowledge of their impermament nature. Emotions flow and ebb in cycles.

  1. Sahasrara, the crown of the head, home of bliss.
  2. Anjana, the temples, center of anger and awareness.
  3. Vishuddha, the throat, center of grief and gratitude.
  4. Anahata, the heart, center of lover, fear and hate.
  5. Manipura, the solar plexus, center of joy and jealousy, greed and generosity.
  6. Swadishthana, the genitals, center of creativity and lust.
  7. Muladhara, the anus, center of inertia and enthusiasm.
Anger descends into grief, grief descends into fear and hate, which in turn yield to greed and jealousy, giving way to lust that brings about inertia. Inertia flips over to enthusiasm, triggering creativity, giving rise to joy and generosity, ascending to love, expanding awareness and attaining bliss. These cycles continue and the mind mistakenly attaches thoughts and moods to people and events, developing strong likes and dislikes, that reinforce the associations in a self-fulfilling prophecy.

But the self is untouched and undivided by these disturbances on the periphery. Like a film that is exposed over and over to impressions, the restless mind bears a jumbled image that occludes the self. The mind that is calm and free of stress is possessed of clarity and at one with untainted self. Remove the husk and the rice is already present inside. It is a game of dropping.

Sadhana, body of practices like kriya, erases impressions and concepts that overshadow the self drop away. The peace and perfection that is our very nature is revealed in glimpses. The guru shows us our highest possibility.

You are peace!
