Ashtavakra Gita

The seven layers of existence are body, breath, mind, intellect, , ego and self.

Assessment:


The crux of the matter is mistaking the impermanent for permanent. The physical body itself is changing. The lining of the stomach is renewed as fast as two days. The cells that make up the skin are replaced every two to three weeks. White blood cells can last from a few days to a little over a week. Red blood cells last for about four months.

Thoughts change. Emotions and moods change, the experience of an incessant stream of events. The experience of change implies a reference point. This is the unchanging, untarnished self. There are seven levels of existence. We can unpack this knowledge by analogy to a computer as follows:

  1. Body, the physical body, is like the hardware.
  2. Breath, the lifeline, is like the electricity that powers on.
  3. Mind is like the Operating System, makes the hardware function as a computer.
  4. Intellect is the programs like spreadheet software, word processor, email and web browser, that make the computer useful.
  5. Memory is the impressions created when programs run.
  6. Ego is the self-awareness. Here, the analogy runs out as a computer is not self-aware.
  7. Self that which the awareness is about.


"You are everything and nothing!"
